Tournament Information

Welcome to the Brisbane Buzzwoles Sunday Tournaments page! This page is the home for all competitive Pokemon Video Game Championships tournaments hosted by us in Queensland, Australia. For the time being, we are unable to host officially sanctioned Play! Pokemon events however we are still able to host unofficial local tournaments for fun!

Team sheet submissions are required for our tournaments, however they do not need to be provided physically. To submit a digital team sheet, please send an email to scrubbs.pokemon@gmail.com with a completed team sheet pdf file attached. Please ensure the subject of your email matches the following format:

Team Submission - (Date of Tournament) - (Your Name)'

e.g.

Team Submission - 27/03/2022 - Damon Murdoch.

This will help me keep track of them if they are needed for team checks. If you have not submitted a digital team sheet, you will be required to submit a physical team sheet on the day. For more information, please see the definitions section for teamsheets below. Please note that digital team sheets are not allowed for official, sanctioned tournaments and are only permissible for our grassroots events.

All of our tournaments will be held in the best-of-three format, in the most recent official Pokemon VGC format unless otherwise specified. Please These tournaments are mostly held just for fun and practise, however prizes may be offered for winners or random participants on the day. However, season points will be awarded to all competitors depending on placement and these will remain until the end of the season. The points breakdown can be seen below.

Definitions

Here are some commonly-used tournament terms which may be confusing for newer players and / or parents. Please feel free to contact me with any other questions you might have or anything you think needs to be clarified!

  • Best of Three

    In a best-of-three tournament, players play a maximum of three games, or until one player has won two games. As opposed to a best of one tournament, where only one match is played each round to decide the winner.

  • Swiss Rounds

    In the swiss rounds of a tournament, you are paired up against a random opponent with the same (or as close as possible) win-loss record as you. You are not eliminated from the tournament if you lose multiple times, and can keep playing until the end of the tournament regardless of your win-loss record.

  • Teamsheet

    A teamsheet is an official document which records all of the significant information about the team you submit for a tournament. For example, the species, items, abilities, etc. for all of your pokemon. For all official Play! Pokemon events, a teamsheet is required to be submitted before round one of the tournament. For some larger events, teamsheets may be submitted online through the official rk9labs platform however for smaller events you will almost always need to fill out a physical teamsheet. Teamsheets are required to ensure players are using legal teams, and that players are not changing teams (or items / moves / etc.) throughout the day. The official Pokemon team sheet template can be found here . Otherwise, you can use the (unofficial) automatic team sheet filler here.

  • Top Cut

    For a tournament that has a top cut, the top-x players in the tournament (usually the top 4 or the top 8, depending on player count) make it into the top cut bracket. Those players then enter into a single elimination (one loss and you are out) bracket until a winner is decided.
